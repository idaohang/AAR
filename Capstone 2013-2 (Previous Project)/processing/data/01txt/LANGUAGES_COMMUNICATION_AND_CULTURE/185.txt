Courses in cross-cultural communication and in &quot;foreign&quot; languages, in Australian universities at least, often fail to communicate across institutional and disciplinary barriers.  Starting from an analysis of two courses in intercultural communication, this article examines how they foreground cultural difference and where they locate it in relation to the classroom.
